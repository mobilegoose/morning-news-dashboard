## 2. Most Useful Development Today

### September 18 is the key date for the new public-charge rule

The final rule can matter to some people seeking admission or adjustment of status, but it **does not itself change eligibility for public benefits**, and many humanitarian categories are exempt. The current 2022 rule remains in effect through September 17. USCIS is expected to issue revised forms and guidance before the change. Anyone potentially affected should avoid dropping health or nutrition benefits based on headlines alone and seek qualified immigration advice. [Read the rule](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2026/07/20/2026-14539/public-charge-ground-of-inadmissibility) · [Read ILRC’s plain-language update](https://www.ilrc.org/public-charge/latest)

### New Free Journal Articles

- **[Communication makes intensive language therapy more efficient: a randomized-controlled trial in chronic post-stroke aphasia](https://academic.oup.com/braincomms/advance-article-pdf/doi/10.1093/braincomms/fcag252/68690737/fcag252.pdf)** — *Brain Communications*, online accepted manuscript posted July 18, 2026. **Evidence: randomized controlled human trial; open-access accepted manuscript.** Forty-four adults with chronic post-stroke aphasia received two weeks of equally intensive therapy (12.5 hours/week): communication-focused Intensive Language Action Therapy or conventional speech-language therapy. The communication-focused group improved more on the language battery, and depression scores also favored that group. **Why it matters:** it suggests how therapy is delivered—not only its intensity—may improve rehabilitation efficiency and mood. **Limits:** small sample, short treatment period, and limited follow-up; results may not generalize beyond chronic aphasia.
- **[Soluble high-molecular-weight amyloid-β species derived from amyloid-β-laden brains induce cerebral β-amyloidosis](https://academic.oup.com/braincomms/article/8/4/fcag188/8728449)** — *Brain Communications*, July 20, 2026. **Evidence: peer-reviewed preclinical mouse and brain-tissue study; open access.** Researchers found that soluble amyloid assemblies larger than 150 kDa from plaque-bearing mouse and human Alzheimer’s brains accelerated amyloid deposition when introduced into susceptible mice; smaller fractions did not. **Why it matters:** it narrows the candidate “seed” species that may drive spread and could inform future biomarkers or drug targets. **Limits:** this is mechanistic animal work, not a treatment trial; it does not show benefit or safety in people.
- **[Duration of Lenalidomide Maintenance in Multiple Myeloma](https://www.nejm.org/doi/full/10.1056/NEJMoa2600157)** — *New England Journal of Medicine*, July 16, 2026. **Evidence: recent phase 3 randomized human trial; free full article.** In 516 people with standard-risk, newly diagnosed multiple myeloma who did not receive upfront autologous stem-cell transplantation, continuous maintenance until progression did not improve seven-year overall survival compared with stopping at two years and caused more toxicity. **Why it matters:** it directly informs the burden-versus-benefit question for long maintenance therapy. **Limits:** this recent fallback lies outside the 72-hour journal window; the findings may not apply to high-risk disease or transplant-treated patients, and treatment decisions remain individualized.
