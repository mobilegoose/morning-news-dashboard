1. Top 5 Things to Know Today

  1. The Iran war put a second major shipping chokepoint at risk. U.S. forces said they completed an eleventh consecutive night of strikes in Iran, while Houthi threats prompted ships to turn away from the Bab el-Mandeb route. With traffic through the Strait of Hormuz already disrupted, the Red Sea threat could further impede oil and global trade. AP: Houthi shipping threat, July 22 · Reuters: latest U.S. strikes, July 22
  2. The administration held back more than $1 billion in Medicaid payments to California and Minnesota. Federal officials cited suspected fraud or noncompliance; the states dispute the action and say they were not given specific evidence. Payments can be restored if claims are validated, but the deferrals could strain providers while disputes proceed. AP, July 22
  3. The House passed a temporary funding bill months before the shutdown deadline. The 220–205 vote would fund the government through December 4 and now sends the bill to a Senate where a bipartisan deal is still needed. AP, July 22
  4. Arizona Republicans nominated prominent 2020-election deniers for two statewide offices. Rep. Andy Biggs won the GOP governor primary and will face Democratic Gov. Katie Hobbs; state Rep. Alex Kolodin won the GOP secretary-of-state primary. These races could affect control of a swing-state government and its election administration. AP official results · Guardian overview, July 22
  5. Social Security added online disability-hearing forms and notices. Claimants with a my Social Security account can now submit recent-treatment, medication, and work-background forms online and receive hearing notices in the Message Center. This is a concrete accessibility and case-management improvement, while paper notices continue. SSA, July 21

2. Most Useful Development Today

New online tools for Social Security disability appeals

People awaiting an ALJ hearing can now complete and submit forms HA-4631 (Recent Medical Treatment), HA-4632 (Medications), and HA-4633 (Work Background) through my Social Security. Hearing notices are also available in the Message Center, optional email or text alerts can flag new notices, and the status tracker now shows more hearing and appeal milestones. Paper notices still arrive by mail, so users should keep both mailing and online contact information current. SSA announcement and instructions

New Free Journal Articles

  • Neurocircuit dysfunction during an emotional interference task in combat veterans with post-traumatic stress and mild traumatic brain injury — Brain Communications, online July 22, 2026. Evidence: peer-reviewed human observational fMRI study. Among 109 combat veterans, greater symptom burden was associated with altered visual-affective and frontal brain activity; PTSD and mild-TBI symptom measures showed different connectivity patterns. This may help refine mechanistic models of overlapping PTSD and brain-injury symptoms, but the cross-sectional veteran sample cannot establish causation or a treatment target, and some secondary comparisons did not survive correction. Free full-text PDF
  • Multi-cohort plasma proteomics identifies ANTXR2 and physical activity–related networks linked to neurodegeneration — Brain Communications, online July 21, 2026. Evidence: peer-reviewed observational, multi-cohort proteomics study. Six datasets included objectively measured activity (n=65), self-reported activity (n=10,644), an exercise intervention (n=654), and dementia or neuropathology validation cohorts. Activity-related protein networks were associated with cognition and neurodegenerative-disease measures, with ANTXR2 emerging across analyses. The work is relevant to Alzheimer's and functional aging, but heterogeneous activity measures and mostly associative analyses do not prove that exercise changes ANTXR2 or prevents dementia. Free full-text PDF
  • Brain-derived extracellular-vesicle microRNAs across Alzheimer's disease and frontotemporal dementia — Brain Communications, online July 21, 2026. Evidence: peer-reviewed exploratory postmortem laboratory study. Investigators studied 25 brain donors across five diagnostic groups, with small-RNA sequencing on three biological samples per group. Several microRNA patterns differed across diagnoses and miR-224-5p remained significant in selected comparisons, but no sequencing candidates survived false-discovery correction. This is early biomarker-generating work—not a blood diagnostic or treatment—and the very small postmortem sample requires independent validation. Free full-text PDF
